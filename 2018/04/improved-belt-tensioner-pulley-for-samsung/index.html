Improved Belt Tensioner Pulley for Samsung

Thu Apr 19, 2018 by Charles Mims

I had the terrible misfortune of buying a Samsung dryer with one critical design flaw and several annoying flaws that aren’t apparent in the store. The critical flaw is the belt tensioner pulley which has a metal-on-metal bearing that wears out after about a year. The first time it happened I replaced it, hoping that the replacement part with the quality grease I used would last longer. It didn’t. This time around, I decided something more extreme was in order. At the suggestion of an IRC buddy, I designed a replacement pulley and printed it in nylon. It has pressed into it 3 608 skateboard bearings, held into the original bracket by a 5⁄16” bolt. I had to drill out the post on the original bracket. It seems to work well, but did require some tweaking of the bracket one way then the other to get it aligned so that the belt didn’t hop off on start-up. I’m guessing I probably tweaked the bracket a little bit while trying to remove the post.
